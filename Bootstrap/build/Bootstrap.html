Toyota has put an end to the story with mysterious teasers: after all, this is the ninth generation of Camry. But why does it look almost the same as its predecessor?

Let's try to understand the logic of the automaker. Sedans are already moving to the rank of dinosaurs - automakers are abandoning them altogether: the Ford Focus has rested, the Passat has not appeared in the ninth generation. However, Toyota apparently knows some secret, because at the end of last year, the Camry became the fifth best-selling car in the United States, losing to the pickups and RAV4 brother beloved by this market. In global sales in 2022, Camry became the third, having found almost 612 thousand customers.

In such a situation, Toyota acted as a medical worker, one of the main rules of which is "do no harm". Reshaping something that sells well would not be quite right. Therefore, the company has only worked on the errors. But did she make a miscalculation?

From a design point of view, we will not consider the car, since here on the face (well, on the stern, excuse the pun) is a banal restyling with the preservation of the main bodywork. Much more interesting is the fact that it has not become news, the rejection of the V6 - one of the most beloved engines of customers in Russia. Now the Toyota Camry is offered only as a hybrid. At least in those markets for which the new product has been announced.

The model is presented with front and all-wheel drive, but in both cases the hybrid is based on a 2.5-liter gasoline engine. The base Camry received two electric motors, the AWD modification has a third motor on the rear axle. The power of the system is 228 and 265 hp, respectively.

By default, the new Toyota Camry is equipped with a keyless start, dual-zone climate control, wireless charging, a Toyota Safety Sense 3.0 complex, a dashboard with a 7-inch display and multimedia with an 8-inch screen (in more expensive versions, the model has a fully digital dashboard on a 12.3-inch display and multimedia of a similar diagonal).

The model will enter the market in the spring of 2024. Prices have not yet been announced, but you can buy an eighth-generation Camry with a hybrid transmission starting from $ 29 thousand. The novelty is likely to be more expensive.
